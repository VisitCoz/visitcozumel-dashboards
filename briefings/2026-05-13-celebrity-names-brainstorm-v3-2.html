Ten names.

"A Mayan Family Day" is too generic — Celebrity needs a name that promises an experience and triggers a click. Below: 5 names built in the Celebrity-catalog pattern (Hidden / Awakening / Authentic / Inside / Discovery framings), and 5 wild ones for the brochure cover hook.

What a sellable Celebrity excursion name does

From scanning Celebrity's Cozumel shelf — beach, ruins, snorkel, culinary. The ones that sell hard share a pattern.
  • Promises transformation, not just description. "Awakening," "Discovery," "Encounter" beat "Visit," "Tour," "Workshop."
  • Has a premium signal. "Hidden," "Authentic," "Behind," "Inside," "Private," "Premium."
  • Pairs concrete with abstract. "Stone & Sky," "Brushes & Cosmos," "X & Y" rhythm reads premium.
  • Names the cultural anchor. "Mayan" cues the buyer; "Cozumel" anchors the place.
  • Family signal when applicable. Not "Family Day" (boring) — but "for every age," "across generations" through the description; the title is allowed to be premium.

Five sellable · Celebrity-pattern

Each follows a tested Celebrity-shelf framing. Brochure-scannable. Buyer reads the title and immediately wants more info.
S1 · awakening
Mayan Awakening · Watercolors & The Cosmos
"Awakening" is one of Celebrity's most repeated premium words. Promises transformation. Then concrete: watercolors + cosmos. Pairs abstract + concrete in 5 words.
tone · premium transformation
S2 · hidden
Cozumel's Hidden Mayan · A Painter's Discovery
"Hidden" is Celebrity-shelf gold (used in Hidden Mayan Treasures, Hidden Paradise, etc.). "Painter's" reads premium without saying "workshop." "Discovery" closes the loop.
tone · premium discovery
S3 · authentic
Authentic Mayan · Brushes & The 360° Cosmos
"Authentic" is the Celebrity guest's favorite word. "Brushes" is concrete + premium. "The 360° Cosmos" signals the dome without naming it. Three premium signals stacked.
tone · authentic + immersive
S4 · behind
Behind the Mayan Sky · A Watercolor Workshop
"Behind the Sky" is a poetic premium hook ("Behind X" pattern from Celebrity's culinary line). "A Watercolor Workshop" is the concrete promise — no ambiguity.
tone · poetic premium
S5 · inside
Inside the Mayan Cosmos · A Painter's Cozumel Day
"Inside" is Celebrity's classic immersive framing. Names both halves: the cosmos (dome) + the painter's day (workshop). The most descriptive of the five.
tone · descriptive premium

Five wild · for the brochure cover

Evocative, mysterious, conversation-starters. Best as cover-image tagline behind whichever sellable name you pick. They don't have to make sense — they have to make the buyer stop scrolling.
W1 · mystical
Where Ixchel Listens
The Moon goddess of San Gervasio (here on Cozumel) hears your guest's wishes. Mythological, intimate, female-coded. Strong with the wellness-curious 50+ buyer.
tone · mystical female
W2 · primal
The Jaguar's Watercolor
Balam, the Mayan jaguar carrying the stars on his skin (named in the dome film), now holding a brush. Primal, strange, photographable.
tone · primal wild
W3 · poetic
Brushes Beneath the Cosmos
A pure image: brushes in the family's hands, the cosmos overhead. Atmospheric. Quiet. Premium.
tone · atmospheric poetic
W4 · provocative
A Brush With the Gods
Double meaning — a brush (paintbrush) + a brush (close call) with the Mayan gods. Slightly cheeky, slightly cinematic.
tone · cheeky cinematic
W5 · mysterious
What the Mayans Saw
Promise + curiosity gap. Three words. Reads as a discovery, demands an answer. Brochure-cover gold.
tone · curiosity gap
