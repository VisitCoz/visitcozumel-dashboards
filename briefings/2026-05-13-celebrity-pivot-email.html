Hi Matthew,

Thank you again for the time you gave us in Cozumel on May 6. We've been working on what to send back, and after some thinking we want to share a different direction than the one we were drafting initially.

You mentioned at Seatrade Miami that Celebrity wants more family-friendly Cozumel options, and we believe the strongest version of what we showed you is this:

A Mayan Family Day · Painting the Cosmos, Seeing the Sun.

It is a 3-hour Mayan day for families and couples. Guests paint a Mayan deity or celestial symbol in watercolor with a Cozumel artist at our family preserve, Tierra Maya — and then look at Kin, the Sun god, directly through a 16-inch solar telescope at Mexico's premier 360° immersive cinema. The painting is the bridge — they paint Kin in the morning and see him in the afternoon. The watercolor goes home with them. The memory of looking at the Sun together stays with the family.

The workshop is led by María Argüero, a professional Cozumel painter whose work centers on the Mayan cosmos. She has prepared six Mayan-themed templates — Kin (the Sun god), Ixchel (the Moon goddess), Noh Ek (Venus), the Mayan calendar wheel, the World Tree, and the cosmic turtle — designed so every guest, from age 6 to 80, finishes a piece they are proud of in 90 minutes. No painting experience required.

Attached is a one-page proposal with the full concept, the day structure, and the artist. Pricing and the full SED format will follow shortly once we close the cost stack with our finance team.

Please let us know your team's reaction — and any feedback on the title, the templates, or the structure. We're flexible on all of it.
