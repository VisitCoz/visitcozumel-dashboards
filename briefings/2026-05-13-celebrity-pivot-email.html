Hi Matthew,

Thank you again for the time you gave us in Cozumel on May 6. After working through what to send back, we want to share a new direction shaped by what you told us at Seatrade Miami about family-friendly Cozumel options.

Beyond the Mayan Sky · A Watercolor Workshop · What the Mayans Saw.

It is a 3-hour Mayan day in three deliberate stops:

Stop one: a quick photo opportunity at Monumentos al Mestizaje on the malecón — guests meet a bronze likeness of Ixchel, the Mayan Moon goddess whose pilgrimage temple stood at San Gervasio, here on Cozumel, for over a thousand years.

Stop two: a short transfer to Tierra Maya, our family preserve in the jungle, where a Cozumel artist leads a 45-minute watercolor workshop. Each guest picks one of eight Mayan-cosmos templates — Kin (the Sun god), Ixchel, Noh Ek (Venus), Balam (the jaguar that carried the stars on his skin), the conch shell, the Mayan calendar wheel, the World Tree, and the cosmic turtle. As they paint, the artist narrates each deity's story. The watercolor goes home with the family.

Stop three: back to town, inside Mexico's premier 360° immersive semisphere. A 60-minute experience: a 20-minute Mayan archaeoastronomy film, then a live professional astronomer narrates the Mayan calendar wheel overhead, names the gods the family just painted, and takes their questions.

The narrative arc: each Mayan deity greets the family three times in one day — in bronze on the malecón, in watercolor in their hands, and on the 360° dome overhead. Guests also learn the Mayan calendar, which is different from the Western calendar — a small "did-you-know" they carry to their dinner table for years.

Attached is the one-page concept proposal. Pricing and the full SED format will follow shortly once we close the cost stack with our finance team.

Please let us know your team's reaction — and any feedback on the title, the templates, the stops, or the structure. We're flexible on all of it.
