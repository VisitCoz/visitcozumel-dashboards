The verdict. Carnival's cultural-with-hands-on shelf tops out at $93.99 retail for the 4.5-hr Mayan ruins + tequila combo. Our $85 net → ~$119–$128 Celebrity retail puts us above every Carnival cultural product except Tulum (which is a 7-hour mainland day with a UNESCO ruins site, not a workshop). We are on a different shelf entirely — premium small-group cultural. There is currently no workshop product on Carnival's catalog that includes a tangible take-home item — that is our white-space differentiator.

What is missing from Carnival's catalog (gaps to brainstorm)

Idea 1
Painting + Sun private family booking
Sell the same product as a private experience for one family (4–8 pax). Carnival's Romantic Getaway at Passion Island shows there is a per-couple/per-private shelf at $205.99. We could offer Painting + Sun private at $599–$799 flat — same costs except the artist+astronomer are now serving one group. Margin doubles.
Target: Celebrity Beyond · Edge-class · suites · honeymooners · multi-gen family bookings
Idea 2
A 2-hour quick-call dome + tasting
Carnival's catalog has very few short products. A 2-hour dome-only product (no workshop, light Mayan tasting at TM, ~$45 retail / ~$35 net) could fit ships with 6-hour port calls or guests who want pier time. Reuses our dome relationship and Joel's TM hosting; no artist needed.
Target: short-call ships · price-sensitive guests · secondary product, not Painting + Sun replacement
Idea 3
Carnival is empty on art
Zero of Carnival's 47 products is art / craft / workshop with a take-home creative piece. The closest is the chocolate workshop (food, eaten, not kept). Painting + Sun is the only take-home-art product on Cozumel. Pitch this to Matt explicitly — it's a category Celebrity does not have on their own shelf either, based on the catalog patterns.
Target: Celebrity Small Group shelf · "no equivalent on competing lines"
Idea 4
A sunset / evening variant
Carnival's $150 Velada astronomical-evening product slot is empty in Cozumel. Our planetarium contact Yesli quoted $150 MXN/pax Velada rate. A 2.5-hr sunset Painting + Stars (paint at golden hour, dome velada) for the rare ships with evening departures. Niche, low volume, high margin.
Target: Celebrity ships with late departure calls · couples premium tier

Final pricing call on Painting + Sun, with the data behind it

Open at $85 USD/pax VAT-incl · Floor $80 · Min 8 / Max 24.

  • Our own internal ladder (HT $74, NK $75) → coherent step up for a 3-hr small-group premium product.
  • Carnival's nearest format match (Chocolate Workshop) is $69.99 retail and Carnival's nearest content match (Mayan Interactive + Planetarium) is $79.99 retail — both mass-market shelves, both inferior in cap-size, art content, and take-home.
  • Carnival's nearest price ceiling for cultural is the 7-hour Tulum at $142.99. Our 3-hour at Celebrity retail $119–$128 fits cleanly below it.
  • At $85 net our 8-pax-min margin is 37%; at max 24 it is 46%. With insurance + contingency loaded, this is the right floor for a contract we will actually want to run.
For Matt's reply. "We have priced this excursion based on what it actually is — a 3-hour small-group product capped at 24 guests, with a named local artist, all watercolor materials, a take-home painting per guest, a 60-minute interactive dome with a live local astronomer, and private van transport. The biggest line in our cost stack is the private van — the operators charge us in Mexican pesos, and with the exchange rate at roughly 16 MXN per USD (it was over 20 a year ago), every transport invoice translates to more dollars on our side than it did. We have priced the experience tight to what it is, not speculatively."
