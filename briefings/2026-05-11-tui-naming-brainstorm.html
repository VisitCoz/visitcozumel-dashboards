Ten new names

The working name is generic. Below: 5 German-market titles (descriptive, scannable, brochure-safe — the kind TUI's audience expects) and 5 wild titles (evocative, mysterious, conversation-starters — good for the thumbnail or the cover image). All 10 are usable; pick the register that fits TUI's portfolio.

Five German-market titles

Direct, descriptive, brochure-scannable. The TUI guest scans these and immediately knows what they're getting. Compound nouns and clear promises — the way German tour copy reads.
G1 · recommended
Beobachter des Universums
"Observers of the Universe — A Mayan Journey through Cozumel"
Directly riffs on the dome film's own title. Highest credibility hit if guests Google. Sub: "Eine Maya-Reise durch Cozumel".
tone · cinematic, premium, film-aligned
G2
Das Geheimnis der Maya
"The Secret of the Maya — Stars, Sun, Gods"
"Geheimnis" (secret) is a strong German tour-copy word. Mysterious without overpromising. Sub: "Sterne, Sonne, Götter".
tone · mysterious, accessible
G3
Unter der Maya-Kuppel
"Under the Mayan Dome — Cozumel and the Universe"
"Unter der Kuppel" leans into the venue. Cozy, evocative, German-natural compound. Sub: "Cozumel und das Universum".
tone · cozy, venue-forward
G4
Die Sterne der Maya
"The Stars of the Maya — How They Read the Sky"
Clean, declarative. The "wie sie den Himmel lasen" subtitle promises the educational content TUI's culture-curious guest wants. Sub: "Wie sie den Himmel lasen".
tone · educational, clean
G5
Kin, Ixchel und die Sterne
"Kin, Ixchel and the Stars — A Mayan Journey"
Names the two key deities up front. German readers love the unfamiliar proper nouns — feels expert, not generic. Sub: "Eine Maya-Reise".
tone · proper-noun premium

Five wild titles

Evocative, mysterious, conversation-starters. Best for the brochure cover or the marketing thumbnail rather than the SED's tour name field. Use as the headline if you want the German guest to stop scrolling.
W1
Der weisse Hase auf dem Mond
"The White Rabbit on the Moon"
Lifted from the dome film: the Maya saw a white rabbit on the Moon — Ixchel's emblem. Beautiful, weird, memorable. Dinner-table story.
tone · poetic mystery
W2
Der Jaguar, der die Sterne trug
"The Jaguar Who Carried the Stars"
Balam, the Mayan jaguar with stars on his skin — the constellation framing for the night sky. Primal, wild, masculine pull.
tone · primal, wild
W3
Wo die Maya hinaufblickten
"Where the Maya Looked Up"
Quiet, atmospheric. The exact spot in Cozumel where the Maya read their sky. Less title, more invitation.
tone · atmospheric, quiet
W4
13.0.13.5.1
(no translation — just the date)
The Mayan Long Count for May 6, 2026. Cryptic, cinematic, makes the buyer ask "what is this?" Works as a sub-headline beneath any G-title.
tone · cryptic, cinematic
W5
Das Auge des Sonnengottes
"The Eye of the Sun God"
Direct reference to the rooftop telescope payoff — looking at Kin himself. Bold, memorable, sets up the climax in the title itself.
tone · bold, climax-forward

Suggested pairings

A SED tour name + a marketing tagline drawn from the wild list = brochure scannable + cover-image hook. My picks:
  • SED name: Beobachter des Universums · cover tagline: Das Auge des Sonnengottes
  • SED name: Das Geheimnis der Maya · cover tagline: Der weisse Hase auf dem Mond
  • SED name: Die Sterne der Maya · cover tagline: Wo die Maya hinaufblickten
