Plaza + Dome · two ways to walk it

Tour A was the dome alone — too close to the pier, too short, hard to charge €45. Tour B adds Plaza de las Dos Culturas as the cultural anchor — the Mayan + Spanish heritage of Cozumel — before (or after) the immersive 360° fulldome experience and the solar telescope. The dome is never called a planetarium in customer copy; it is a "premier 360° immersive cinema." Below: two routing options, both 2.5 hours, with the recommended pick.
your reality check

2 hours is too short for €45. The dome alone is a 60-min experience near the pier — guests would feel the price is high for what they got. Adding Plaza de las Dos Culturas extends the tour to 2.5 hours, adds a cultural walking layer, and gives a tangible "second experience" that justifies the ticket. Same one bus, same throughput (3 departures per ship call), better perceived value.

"Planetarium" is never used in customer copy. We call it: "Mexico's premier 360° immersive cinema" · "the 360° fulldome theater" · "the semisphere". Never the institutional name.

Public-facing tour description (for SED R118)

Maya in Cozumel · Plaza, Sterne & Sonne

Two and a half hours through the Mayan world of Cozumel — beginning on foot, ending under the dome of the sky itself.

Begin your tour at Plaza de las Dos Culturas, the historic heart of Cozumel where Mayan and Spanish-Mexican cultures met. Your guide walks you through the plaza, the kiosk, and the monuments that mark the meeting of two worlds — and tells the story of Ixchel, the Mayan Goddess of the Moon, whose pilgrimage temple at San Gervasio drew Maya seekers from across the peninsula for over a thousand years.

Then step inside Mexico's premier 360° immersive fulldome cinema. The official 18-minute film Mayan Archaeoastronomy: Observers of the Universe tours six great Mayan temples — San Gervasio (here on Cozumel), Chichén Itzá, Uxmal, Edzná, Palenque, and Bonampak — and shows how each was built as a calendar in stone, aligned to the Sun, the Moon, and Venus. A feast of colors and sounds in stunning 360° visuals — like being inside a beautiful stylized painting. After the film, our astronomy guide writes your own birthday in the Mayan Long Count and surfaces the sky as it stood the day you were born.

The climax comes on the rooftop. Through a 16-inch solar telescope with a professional safety filter, you look directly at the Sun — Kin, the Sun god the Maya invented their entire world to honor — safely magnified into the field of view of your own eye. A short walk back to the cruise pier closes the day.

All-weather; the only sun-dependent element is the rooftop telescope. Inclusive-destination certified.
