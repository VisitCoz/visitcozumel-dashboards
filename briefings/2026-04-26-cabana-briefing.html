Context · why we're doing this

The opportunity, in plain words.

Celebrity has dozens of Cozumel excursions, but no fully private cabana on its shelf. Their existing beach products — Kuza VIP, La Casa, Barracuda — are all shared beach clubs at $137–209 per guest. We have access to Cabana Beach (Tan Navarro's venue at the south end of Chankanaab Park). Tan does not market to cruise lines directly. That's our wedge.

Why now

Mike pitched this to Matthew Kaye and Adam Cullen at Seatrade Miami on April 15. They were receptive. The window to submit a formal Tour Content Worksheet is open — Native Kitchen took ~3 weeks from pitch to approval, and we want to move before momentum fades.

Why two products, not one

One product covers the budget guest, one covers the premium guest. Beach Club competes with La Casa and Barracuda on price. Private Cabana owns the white space — nothing else on Celebrity's shelf is fully private. Two SKUs gives Celebrity flexibility to upsell across guest types.

Why per-pax for one, per-cabana for the other

Beach Club is shared — strangers fill a van together — so per-pax pricing is natural. Private Cabana is exclusive — one party per cabana — so flat per-cabana pricing protects the privacy promise. A couple pays the same as a family of six. That's the deal.

Why Cabana Beach

Tan offers wholesale rates: cabana 50% off retail, food package 25% off, park entry direct to Mike at $20/pax (vs $29 retail). Twelve cabanas at the venue — small enough to feel exclusive, big enough to scale. Existing Tikin Xic-style food, beach access, snorkel, all built in.

What this means commercially. If Celebrity approves both, we're looking at meaningful incremental revenue per Celebrity sailing day with healthy margins (31% on Beach Club, 41–65% on Cabana for typical party sizes). Across a season, this is six-figure revenue from a single account.

Product A · Catalog listing

Cozumel Hideaway: Beach Club & Caribbean Feast

A relaxed beach day at Cozumel's most exclusive private venue, with lunch and open bar included.

Trade the cruise pier for a tucked-away corner of Chankanaab Park — Cozumel's most celebrated marine sanctuary — at a venue not marketed to other cruise lines. Just 15 minutes from your ship, your private air-conditioned van delivers you to a small, palm-shaded retreat where the day unfolds at your own pace.

Snorkel directly off the reef with no boat ride and no crowds. Stretch out on a sun lounger steps from the Caribbean. Wander the Chankanaab lagoon. When hunger calls, your group gathers for a plated lunch under the palms — fresh fish, ceviche, guacamole, handmade tortillas — paired with an open bar of soft drinks, ice-cold beer, and Mexican spirits.

Your bilingual host stays with the group from pier to pier, handling logistics so you don't have to. This is a small-group experience designed for guests who want the warmth of Cozumel without the noise of the typical beach club.

Cozumel Hideaway: Private Cabana & Caribbean Feast

Your own cabana on Cozumel's most exclusive private beach — for couples, families, or a small group of friends.

Step away from everything. Cozumel Hideaway: Private Cabana reserves a fully private beach cabana — yours alone for the day, no sharing — at a small Chankanaab retreat that does not appear on any other cruise line's shelf. From 1 to 6 guests, the cabana is yours; the price is the same.

A private air-conditioned van delivers you to the venue 15 minutes from the cruise pier. Inside the cabana — shaded, breezy, steps from the water — your group settles in with a plated Caribbean lunch and open bar. Snorkel off the reef. Float in the natural Chankanaab lagoon. Disappear into the rhythm of the island for an afternoon.

The cabana includes everything: private transfer, dedicated bilingual host, park entrance, snorkel gear, towels, lunch, and open bar (soft drinks, beer, domestic spirits). For honeymoons, anniversaries, family days, or a small celebration, this is the most private beach experience Celebrity offers in Cozumel.
