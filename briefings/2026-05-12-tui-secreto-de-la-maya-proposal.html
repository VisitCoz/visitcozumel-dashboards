Discover the secret world of the Maya in just two hours — from the historic plaza of Cozumel to a 360° immersive dome that brings the Mayan sky to life.

Your guests' journey begins on foot at Plaza de las Dos Culturas, the heart of Cozumel where Mayan and Spanish-Mexican cultures first met. Your English-speaking guide walks the group through the kiosk and historic monuments, and tells the story of Ixchel — the Mayan Moon Goddess, whose pilgrimage temple at San Gervasio drew Maya seekers from across the peninsula for over a thousand years.

A short photo stop at Cozumel's historic aircraft monument adds a quick photo opportunity on the way to the next experience.

Then guests step inside Mexico's premier 360° fulldome cinema for a 20-minute immersive film about Mayan archaeoastronomy. The film tours six great Mayan temples — San Gervasio on Cozumel, Chichen Itza, Uxmal, Edzna, Palenque, and Bonampak — showing how each was built as a calendar in stone, aligned to the Sun god Kin, the Moon, and Venus. A feast of colors and sounds in stunning 360° visuals.

The highlight of the day follows: a 25-minute interactive live segment with our astronomy guide. Volunteer guests' birthdays are translated into the Mayan Long Count, and the dome projects the actual night sky over Cozumel as it stood the day each guest was born. The most personalised moment of the day.

A short walk back to the cruise pier closes the tour. All-weather indoor cinema. Not a beach tour — a journey to what the Maya themselves saw when they looked at the sky.

The two stops · the day in two acts

A Mayan day in two stops.

A guided cultural walk through historic Cozumel, then an immersive cosmic finale under a 360° fulldome cinema — all within walking distance of the cruise pier. The full Mayan world, condensed into two well-chosen acts.

Act 1 · Plaza de las Dos Culturas. A 35-minute guided walking tour through the historic Cozumel plaza where Mayan and Spanish-Mexican cultures met. Your English-speaking guide tells the story of Ixchel, the Mayan Moon Goddess, and the pilgrimage temple of San Gervasio that drew Maya from across the Yucatán peninsula for over a thousand years. Photo stops at the kiosk and surrounding monuments. A brief stop at Cozumel's historic aircraft monument on the way to the next experience.

Act 2 · The 360° fulldome cinema. A 20-minute Mayan archaeoastronomy film tours six great temples — San Gervasio (here on Cozumel), Chichén Itzá, Uxmal, Edzná, Palenque, and Bonampak — showing how each was built as a calendar in stone, aligned to the Sun god Kin, the Moon, and Venus. A feast of colors and sounds in stunning 360° visuals.

The highlight. A 25-minute interactive live segment with our astronomy guide. Volunteer guests' birthdays are translated into the Mayan Long Count, and the dome projects the actual night sky over Cozumel as it stood the day each guest was born. The Mayan calendar wheel comes alive overhead.

Three naming directions · the brochure name

Let us know which name fits your shelf.

We've developed three name directions for the brochure title. Each is designed to convert for TUI's culture-curious German guest. We are flexible — the goal is a name that resonates and sells on your shelf.

1. Das Geheimnis der Maya

Sterne, Sonne, Tempel. "The Secret of the Maya: Stars, Sun, Temples." Concrete content + mystery hook + secular tone. Three German compound nouns the audience scans instantly.

2. Beobachter des Universums

"Observers of the Universe." Riffs directly on the dome film's own title. Highest credibility hit if guests look it up. Cinematic and premium.

3. Die Sterne der Maya

Wie sie den Himmel lasen. "The Stars of the Maya: How They Read the Sky." Educational angle — matches TUI's Bildungsreise brand tone.

We are flexible — the goal is a name that converts for TUI's guest. We welcome your team's input on the brochure title and on any of the operational specifications.
