The Celebrity onboarding kit.

All 15 documents are saved under Projects/Matthew_Visit_2026-05-06/celebrity_onboarding_kit/ in five buckets. Below: what each one is, my take, and the four things that need to happen before we can fill the Tour Content Worksheet for Matt.

The four blockers, before we fill anything

BLOCKER #1 · Insurance. Celebrity requires $2M General Liability per incident, with an AM Best A:XII or better rated carrier, naming Royal Caribbean Cruises Ltd. as additional insured. Most Mexican carriers do not meet AM Best A:XII. Verify TODAY with your current insurance broker whether we already meet this, and what the gap costs.
BLOCKER #2 · D&B Number. The Tour Operator Profile asks for our Dun & Bradstreet number. Does Visit Cozumel Mexico, S. de R.L. de C.V. have one? If not, registration is free but takes 7–14 days. Check with Adela; if no, register today at dnb.com.
BLOCKER #3 · Cancellation policy in writing. The Contact Form asks for a formal cancellation policy. We need one paragraph defining what happens at min-pax shortfall, weather, no-show, cruise-side cancellation. This should be a written page in our company docs, not just verbal practice.
BLOCKER #4 · María's name + photo consent. Image releases require an Appearance Release signed by every recognizable person in marketing photos. If we name and show María in the Celebrity submission, the Doña Marta lesson applies. Decide before we submit: name + photo, or keep her anonymous (no face shots in submitted photos).

Document-by-document analysis

Category A · Insurance & Compliance

insurance_and_compliance/Tour Operator Insurance Requirements - RCG.pdf
Tour Operator Insurance Requirements Blocker
The hard floor. Read this twice. Most operators get rejected here.
Royal Caribbean Group's minimum insurance specs. For Land Tours: $2M General Liability per incident. For Transportation (Caribbean): $2M auto liability per occurrence for vehicles seating 15 or less. Carrier must be AM Best A:XII or better. Must list RCG and affiliates as additional insured. Must include waiver of subrogation + primary and non-contributory endorsement. Coverage cannot be eroded by defense costs. 30-day cancellation notice required. Tour operator's policy must also cover liability arising from subcontractor operations — meaning María, Yesli's planetarium, and our van vendor must all be covered under our umbrella, OR we need certificates from each of them.

Category B · Brand Guidelines

brand_guidelines/Celebrity Brand Deck V2 (1).pdf
Celebrity Brand Deck V2 Perfect fit
Validates everything about Painting + Sun. Read this, then lift the vocabulary into our worksheet.
Celebrity is "a relaxed luxury resort at sea." Their guest persona is the "Culturally Curious Indulger" with four sub-personas — The Foodie · The Cultural Enthusiast · The Maverick · The Indulgence Seeker. Our product targets The Cultural Enthusiast almost word-for-word: "It's important to you to learn about the rich history of any place you're visiting & visit important historical sites." Celebrity emphasizes Destination · Culinary · Service · Details · Wellbeing. The Edge and Solstice fleets are 82–85% verandas & suites — premium guests. This deck argues our $80 price is actually conservative — their guests pay for craft, details, and culture.

Category C · Templates to fill

templates/Copy of Tour Content Worksheet Cel.xlsx
Tour Content Worksheet Cel Fill next
The big one. 174 rows across 3 sections + a Control tab with dropdowns.
Three sections: (1) Tour Operator + Basic Info (port, country, tour name, duration, min/max, date submitted) · (2) Restrictions (activity level mild/moderate/strenuous, waivers, consumer interests, headsets, age/weight/height restrictions, medical/pregnancy, what to bring/wear) · (3) Tour Content (timetable per stop — driving time, time at site, walking distance in yards, steps, terrain, wheelchair access, ramps, elevators, restrooms, charges). Plus meal/snack/beverage detail. Critical finding from the Control sheet: Celebrity uses a "Small Group" consumer-interest tag — that is our slot. "Family" is also a real tag — supports the family-discount angle. We can pick up to 2 tags.
templates/Pricing Sheet Template - CEL.xlsx
Pricing Sheet Template Fill next
Cleaner than the worksheet. One line per tour, fixed or tiered.
Columns: Port · Brand · Tour Code · Tour Title · Minimum · Currency · Adult (age 13+) · Child (age 4-12) · Infant (age 0-3) · Duration · Notes. Sample shows tiered pricing dropping with group size — min 20 = $78, min 25 = $75, min 30 = $72, min 35 = $70. Two strategic insights: (1) we could offer tiered pricing to push Matt toward higher minimums in exchange for a lower rate — e.g., $80 at min 8, $76 at min 12, $72 at min 20. (2) Infant rate is typically free in our existing HT/NK data — consistent with the template structure.
templates/Subcontractor Tour Details File.xlsx
Subcontractor Tour Details Fill next
Where we declare every vendor: van, dome, artist.
Asks: liquor provided? ground transportation? vehicle capacity? distance? air? water? is this tour subcontracted? operating model? Then four subcontractor blocks each with Type · Company Name · Email Address. For Painting + Sun we declare: Subcontractor 1 = Transfer / [Van vendor name] · Subcontractor 2 = Cultural & Historical / Planetario Cha'an Ka'an / Yesli's email · Subcontractor 3 = Cultural & Historical / María Argüero / her email · Subcontractor 4 = Cultural & Historical / Tierra Maya / Joel's email. Note: María's name will show up here regardless of how we name her in marketing copy.
templates/Tour Operator Contact Form - 2025.xlsx
Tour Operator Contact Form Fill next
Two sheets. Contact info is easy; Entity Information is RFP-grade.
Sheet 1 (Contact Information) is straightforward: company name, DBA, legal entity type, mailing address, primary email per function (tour counts, invoices, allotments, content approval, Riskonnect/Insurance), phone numbers, primary and secondary contacts. Sheet 2 (Entity Information) is much harder: HQ location, notice address, Dun & Bradstreet number, holding company structure, Federal Tax ID, local offices, years in business, pending lawsuits, upcoming mergers, diversity supplier certifications (MBE, MWBE, Disabled, Veteran), top 5 clients, relevant tour-operator experience, cancellation policy in writing, and a clause that they may require audited financial statements for the award.
templates/Tour Operator Profile.doc
Tour Operator Investment Profile Fill next
Background questionnaire. Most checkboxes; opportunity to flag GSTC & exclusivity.
D&B number, founding date, number of offices, private/public, RCCL port-agent status, other cruise lines worked with in this port, RCCL history. Then business relations (affiliations, partnerships, exclusive rights to products/venues — Painting + Sun with María is exclusive to us, flag it), equipment owned (van? probably no — we subcontract), GSTC / sustainability certifications (opportunity to mention Tierra Maya's preserve and the small-group cap), port agent and ground operator ports, kiosk/voucher/hop-on partnerships.

Category D · Image / Media Requirements

image_requirements/Shore-X Media Requirements - Images.docx
Shore Excursion Media Requirements Affects photo selection
Strict but clear. Our proposal photos were not built to this spec.
Technical: JPG/TIF/HEIC · 500KB+ · minimum 2000 × 1440 px (horizontal) · descriptive filenames using dashes. Creative: horizontal only, centered subject, no branding/logos/typography/graphic elements, sharp, not blurry. Quantity: up to 6 used, 10 submittable. People: avoid branded clothing, inappropriate swimwear, tattoos. Legal: every identifiable person needs an Appearance Release; 1 Materials Use form per image. This means the Ixchel monument group shot we love — with branded cruise lanyards visible — may need to be cropped/reshot. Also: the Ixchel illustration we used as a hero in the proposal cannot be submitted — it has graphic-design elements.
image_requirements/Shore-x-media-visual-guide-web.pdf
Shore-X Media Visual Guide Reference
Picture-book version of the requirements doc above. Look at the rejected examples.
Shows approved vs rejected examples. Rejected categories: images with logos/typography, overly dark/complex compositions, blurry/pixelated images, close-ups, composited or photoshopped people. Approved: centered subject, wide horizontal composition, clean light, natural composition. Worth one quick read before we pick our 6 hero photos.

Category E · Legal releases (collect from guests + photo sources)

image_requirements/Appearance Release - TYPE 1 - Shore Ex Template.pdf
Appearance Release TYPE 1 For our own photo shoots
Use when Visit Cozumel is the photographer/producer. One per recognizable person.
Names Tour Operator (us) as "Producer." Guest grants irrevocable, royalty-free, perpetual right to use likeness for any lawful purpose worldwide. Includes minor consent block. Florida law. Every guest in a marketing photo we shoot needs this signed before we submit the image. Practically: bring printed copies + clipboard to every photo-shoot day.
image_requirements/Appearance Release - TYPE 2 - Shore Ex Template.pdf
Appearance Release TYPE 2 For RCG-commissioned shoots
Use only when Royal Caribbean Cruises Ltd. itself is the photographer.
Same legal frame as T1 but names RCG as Producer instead of us. For 99% of our submissions, we use T1.
image_requirements/Materials Use Agreement - TYPE 1 - Shore Ex Template.pdf
Materials Use Agreement TYPE 1 We own the images
One per submitted image where we are the sole copyright owner.
We license the image to RCG "royalty-free, irrevocable, sublicensable and perpetual" with "unlimited use in any media for any purpose whatsoever." We waive the right to inspect or approve any use. We indemnify RCG for any claims arising from the materials. Exhibit A = the materials, Exhibit B = signed appearance releases for every recognizable person. Use this for every photo Adela or we shot at Tierra Maya, the dome, or the malecón.
image_requirements/Materials Use Agreement - TYPE 2 - Shore Ex Template.pdf
Materials Use Agreement TYPE 2 Licensed from third party
For stock photos or images we licensed from a photographer.
Same indemnification frame as T1 but Exhibit B is the license agreement we hold with the photographer/stock site — not appearance releases. For Painting + Sun we probably don't need this if all photos are ours. The Ixchel illustration we used in the proposal may be the only third-party piece — and even that we should not submit (graphic-design element — rejected by media spec).

What I think · the strategic picture

The good news: the Brand Deck and the Worksheet's "Small Group" consumer-interest tag tell us Celebrity has a shelf built exactly for what we are selling. The product is on-brand. The price ($80 adult / $56 child) is conservative for the "Culturally Curious Indulger" persona — Celebrity guests pay for craft. We are not asking them to invent a category.

The grind: the kit is 15 documents because RCG runs a vendor-onboarding process closer to an RFP than a partnership pitch. Top-5 clients, pending lawsuits, D&B numbers, audited financials — this is supply-chain risk management, not creative review. Most of it is data entry; the blocker is insurance.

The hidden cost we hadn't priced: we previously added $3/pax for insurance in our cost stack. With the AM Best A:XII requirement and the obligation to cover subcontractors under our umbrella, our actual premium may be $2–5/pax higher than that. Once you have a quote from the broker, we can adjust the floor.

The thing that will surprise Mario: Section 2 of the Contact Form asks if any of our affiliated entities have pending lawsuits or upcoming mergers/acquisitions. Standard RFP boilerplate, but worth running by him before we answer.
