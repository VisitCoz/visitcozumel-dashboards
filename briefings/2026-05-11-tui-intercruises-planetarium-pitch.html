One bus. Many departures. The planetarium.

Customer Highlights already eats three of our buses. We have one more bus to put in play. This brief proposes a single new TUI tour — Maya-Astronomie at the Planetarium — that uses that one bus to run three departures per ship call, serves up to 135 passengers per call, and uses the same dome + telescope assets we already negotiated for Celebrity. Plus what Yesli told us, plus the bird-watching option with Carolina Can, plus the exact pitch to send intercruises today.

The pitch in one paragraph

read this first

TUI's "Full Culture and Beach" (MXCZM016) is a 4.5-hour Maya tour with a 90-min San Gervasio visit + a beach. Many of TUI's German guests want the culture, not the beach. We propose Maya-Astronomie unter der Kuppel — a 2-hour Mayan-astronomy excursion in Cozumel's planetarium dome, with the official 18-min film Mayan Archaeoastronomy: Observers of the Universe (German dub available), a live calendar-wheel segment, and a guided 16-inch solar telescope viewing of the Sun. One bus, three departures per ship call, up to 135 pax/call. Walking distance from the cruise pier means low logistics risk. Weather-proof: the dome runs in any weather, the telescope is the only sun-dependent piece.

Why one bus works

  • The planetarium is 1/3 mile (≈540 m) from the cruise pier. The bus is for comfort, not necessity. If a bus is delayed, guests can walk.
  • Each 2-hr tour fits in a 9.5-hr port-day three times with buffer windows between departures.
  • The dome holds 95 passengers per show. 45 fits easily, with capacity for the German-speaking guide + driver + escort.
  • The film is available in German. Arqueomaya has a verified German dub — a real differentiator for TUI's German-flag itineraries.
  • All-weather product. Dome runs rain or shine. Solar telescope is the only weather-dependent piece — on overcast days, the time becomes a second dome film or extended calendar-wheel segment.

Optional birding add-on (Tour B)

For TUI guests wanting more depth (the "longer-stay" cohort), a third-departure variant could add a 30-min interpretive sendero with Carolina Can ("Pajariano Cozumar"), Cozumel's bird specialist, walking the planetarium grounds for endemic species. Total: 2.5 hr. Slightly higher price (≈ €55). Run as the late-afternoon "premium" departure when bird activity is higher.

Maya-Astronomie unter der Kuppel · Mayan Astronomy at the Planetarium

For two hours, step inside Cozumel's premier planetarium — one of the most advanced fulldome theaters in Mexico — and meet the Maya through their own astronomy. Watch Mayan Archaeoastronomy: Observers of the Universe, an 18-minute fulldome experience narrated in German that tours six great Maya temples — San Gervasio (here on Cozumel), Chichén Itzá, Uxmal, Edzná, Palenque, and Bonampak — and shows how each was built as a calendar in stone, aligned to the Sun, the Moon, and Venus.

After the film, our astronomy guide writes your own birthday in the Mayan Long Count, and surfaces the night sky as it stood the day you were born. Then climb to the rooftop observatory for a guided viewing of the Sun — Kin, the Sun god the Maya invented their world to honor — through a 16-inch solar telescope, safely magnified through a professional filter.

A short walk back to the cruise pier closes the tour. Indoor and outdoor; the dome runs in any weather, only the telescope is sun-dependent. Inclusive-destination certified for guests with limited mobility.
